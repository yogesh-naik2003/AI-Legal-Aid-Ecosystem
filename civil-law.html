Property Title Transfer

The process of legally changing property ownership requires meticulous due diligence to ensure the title is clear. It involves pre-registration verification, formal documentation, and post-registration updates.

Key Terminology

Title Deed vs Sale Deed Title establishes history; Sale Deed confirms current transfer.
Encumbrance Cert (EC) Evidence that the property has no pending legal/monetary liabilities.

Procedural Flow

  1. Agreement to Sell: Initial commitment on price and terms.
  2. Stamp Duty Payment: Calculated on Market Value or Consideration Value.
  3. Registration: Signed at the Sub-Registrar's office with witnesses.
  4. Mutation: Updating government revenue records post-sale.

Rental Agreements

A robust agreement protects both parties under the Indian Contract Act. Most residential contracts are "Leave and License" agreements for 11 months to avoid strict rent control laws.

Essential Clauses

  • • Lock-in Period: Minimum stay duration preventing early exit.
  • • Security Deposit: Refund conditions and deduction rules.
  • • Maintenance: Minor (Tenant) vs Major (Landlord) repairs.

Dispute Management

  • • Notice Period: Usually 30 days written notice.
  • • Eviction Grounds: Non-payment, misuse, or subletting.
  • • Registration: Mandatory for leases >12 months.

Inheritance & Succession

Succession laws depend on whether the deceased left a Will (Testate) or not (Intestate), and their religion.

Type Governing Act Key Requirement
Testate (With Will) Indian Succession Act, 1925 Probate (for certain assets/regions)
Intestate (Hindu) Hindu Succession Act, 1956 Legal Heir Certificate / Succession Cert
Intestate (Muslim) Sharia (Personal Law) Fixed shares for heirs

Filing a Civil Suit

Governed by the Civil Procedure Code (CPC). Before filing, ensure you are within the Statute of Limitation and have established a clear Cause of Action.

  • Legal Notice: Mandatory in many cases before suing.
  • Jurisdiction: Ensure court has Territorial & Pecuniary authority.
  • Court Fees: Percentage of claim value (Stamp Duty).

Easements & Rights of Way

Rights to use another's land for a specific purpose (e.g., a path). A Prescriptive Easement is acquired by 20 years of continuous, open use. Disputes are usually resolved via an Injunction Suit.

Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

Arbitration

Formal process. The arbitrator's decision (Award) is binding like a court decree.

Mediation

Voluntary. A neutral third party helps parties reach their own settlement.

Conciliation

Conciliator plays an active role, proposing terms of settlement.
