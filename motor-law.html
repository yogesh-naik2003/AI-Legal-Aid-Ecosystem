Detailed information on mandatory vehicle documentation, key traffic offenses, and the entire process of seeking compensation through the **Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT)**.

Mandatory Vehicle Documents and Traffic Penalties

The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (MV Act) mandates essential documentation and safety rules. The 2019 Amendment significantly increased fines to promote stricter adherence and road safety.

Essential Documents (Sections 3 & 39, MV Act)

  • Driving License (DL): **Mandatory** for driving. Driving without a license carries a fine of up to **₹5,000**.
  • Registration Certificate (RC): All vehicles must be registered with the RTO. Must be carried/accessible.
  • Third-Party Insurance: **Compulsory** for all motor vehicles. Driving without valid insurance (Section 146) carries a fine of **₹2,000** (first offence).
  • PUC Certificate: **Pollution Under Control** Certificate is mandatory for checking vehicle emissions compliance.

Key Violations and Penalties

  1. Drunk Driving (Sec. 185): Permissible limit is **30mg alcohol per 100ml blood**. First offense fine is **₹10,000** and/or 6 months imprisonment.
  2. Not Wearing Helmet/Seat Belt: Fine of **₹1,000** and/or community service.
  3. Over Speeding (Sec. 112): Fines are significantly increased, varying by the class of vehicle (LMV or Heavy Vehicle).
  4. Dangerous Driving (Sec. 184): Fine up to **₹5,000** and/or imprisonment.

MACT: The Specialized Accident Compensation Court

The Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT), established under **Section 165** of the MV Act, is a specialized quasi-judicial body created to provide quicker, dedicated adjudication of accident compensation claims than traditional Civil Courts.

Who Can File a Claim (Sec. 166)

  • The injured person.
  • The owner of the damaged property.
  • Legal representatives of the deceased person.

Jurisdiction & Time Limit

  1. Jurisdiction: The claim can be filed before the MACT that covers the area where: (a) the **accident occurred**, (b) the **claimant resides**, OR (c) the **defendant (insurer/owner) resides**.
  2. Time Limit: Claims must ideally be filed within **six months** from the date of the accident. While the Tribunal has discretion to condone delays, timely filing is highly recommended.

Procedural Flow: Filing and Adjudication at MACT

Filing a compensation petition (**Claim Petition** under Sec. 166) requires meticulous documentation, especially concerning medical expenses and loss of income. The process is adversarial but simplified compared to civil litigation.

Mandatory Steps Post-Accident

  1. Immediate Reporting: File an **FIR** (First Information Report) with the police. The police also prepare a **Detailed Accident Report (DAR)** for the MACT.
  2. Seek Medical Aid: Obtain and retain all original medical bills, prescriptions, and disability certificates.
  3. Inform Insurer: Notify your own insurer (for own-damage claim) and the third-party insurer (for liability claim).
  4. Filing Petition: Submit the Claim Petition to the relevant MACT with supporting documents (FIR, medical records, income proof, insurance details).
  5. Notice & Written Statement: The MACT issues **Notice** to the respondents (Driver, Owner, and Insurer), who then file their **Written Statement** contesting the claim/liability.
  6. Evidence & Award: Parties present evidence (witnesses, doctors) and the Tribunal determines liability and the final compensation **Award**.

Crucial Documents Needed for Filing

  • Copy of the **FIR** and **DAR**.
  • Original medical bills, discharge summary, and **Disability Certificate** (if applicable).
  • Proof of age, occupation, and monthly income of the injured/deceased.
  • Vehicle Registration and Insurance policy copy.

How Compensation is Calculated (Loss of Dependency)

Compensation for death or permanent disability is calculated based on complex legal formulas established by Supreme Court judgments, focusing primarily on the economic loss suffered by the victim's family (Loss of Dependency).

Calculation Components in Fatal Cases

The total compensation is the sum of Pecuniary and Non-Pecuniary losses:

  • Loss of Dependency (Pecuniary Loss): **(Annual Income - Personal Expenses) x Multiplier.**
    • **Future Prospects:** A percentage is mandatorily added to the income (e.g., 50% for under 40, salaried) to account for future promotions/increments.
    • **Deduction for Personal Expenses:** A mandatory percentage (1/3 to 1/4) is deducted based on the number of dependents.
  • Non-Pecuniary Heads: Fixed sums for funeral expenses, loss of estate, and **Loss of Consortium** (loss of spousal companionship).
  • Multiplier: A factor based on the age of the deceased/injured, ranging from 5 (for the oldest) to 18 (for the youngest).

Statutory No-Fault Liability (Sec. 164)

The MV Act also provides for fixed minimum compensation on a no-fault basis (without proving negligence) in case of death (**₹5 Lakh**) or grievous hurt (**₹2.5 Lakh**). Claimants must choose between this no-fault system and the full compensation claim (Sec. 166).

Claims in Hit-and-Run and Uninsured Cases

Victims are protected even when the vehicle is untraceable or violates the compulsory insurance law. The government provides relief funds for such vulnerable situations.

The Solatium Fund (Hit-and-Run)

  • Definition: A **Hit-and-Run** case is where the identity of the motor vehicle or the driver cannot be ascertained despite reasonable efforts.
  • Compensation: Victims/families receive fixed compensation from the **Solatium Fund** (government fund): **₹2 Lakh** for death and **₹50,000** for grievous hurt.

Uninsured Vehicles

If the offending vehicle is identified but found to be uninsured, the claim against the driver and owner remains valid. The MACT will pass the award against the driver/owner directly, who is then personally liable to pay the full compensation amount.

Duties of a Driver and Owner Post-Accident

The MV Act imposes specific legal and moral duties on the driver and owner of the vehicle involved in an accident, most importantly providing aid and reporting the incident.

Duties Under Section 134, MV Act

  1. Provide Medical Aid: The driver must take all reasonable steps to secure medical attention for the injured person. This is part of the **Good Samaritan** law protection.
  2. Report to Police: The driver must report the accident to the nearest police station within **24 hours**.
  3. Provide Information: The owner/driver must provide the name and address of the injured person and details of the vehicle to the police or the insurer.

Owner's Liability

The owner is jointly and severally liable with the driver for the compensation paid to the victim (vicarious liability). Furthermore, the insurance company can legally deny the claim if the owner knowingly allowed a person **without a valid driving license** to operate the vehicle at the time of the accident.
