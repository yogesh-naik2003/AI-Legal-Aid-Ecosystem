Understanding the Types:

Pecuniary Jurisdiction: Determined by the monetary value of the claim. This ensures cases are distributed correctly across District and High Courts.

Note for MACT Claims

The MACT (Motor Accident Claims Tribunal) is a specific tribunal. Claims can be filed where the accident occurred, where the claimant resides, or where the defendant (owner/insurer) resides. **Monetary value is irrelevant** for MACT, as MACT has unlimited pecuniary jurisdiction.

Note for Cheque Bounce Cases

Cheque bounce is a **Criminal Offense** (Sec 138 NI Act). The complaint must be filed in the Judicial Magistrate's court located where the **payee's bank branch** (where the cheque was presented) is situated.
